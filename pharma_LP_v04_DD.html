The real challenge

Your loyalty database is growing.
Your basket value isn't.

Pharmacy and OTC retail has six structural problems that prevent CRM from delivering results — and most marketing teams treat them with the same generic newsletter. Here's what's actually bleeding revenue.

60%+

Customers research OTC products online but still buy in-branch — and you have no way to connect the two.

Most OTC purchase decisions start on your e-shop or a health comparison site, but are completed in the pharmacy. Without a unified CDP, the pharmacist doesn't know who they're serving, and your CRM never attributes the in-store revenue to any digital touchpoint.

Seasonal

Allergy, flu, and vitamin season repeat every year — and most CRM teams still run manual campaigns.

Q1 vitamin D, spring allergies, summer travel health kits, winter flu season. These are predictable, high-value windows. Without automated seasonal triggers linked to your OTC category data, you're manually planning what should run on autopilot — and losing first-mover advantage to the pharmacy chain next door.

30–50%

of pharmacy loyalty databases are unusable — duplicates, expired consents, or inactive for 2+ years.

Mergers, POS migrations, and paper-to-digital transitions leave pharmacy databases full of noise. Every message sent to these records harms deliverability and wastes budget — while genuinely valuable chronic care customers get lost in the clutter.

1 NL

You're sending the same newsletter to the chronic patient, the wellness shopper, and the first-time buyer.

The pensioner managing blood pressure medication. The young athlete buying protein and recovery products. The new mother looking for infant care essentials. One newsletter, three completely different health journeys and zero personalisation. Irrelevant messages erode trust — in a category where trust is everything.

Vendor €€€

Pharma vendors want data and co-marketing. You're leaving vendor budget on the table.

OTC manufacturers have significant co-marketing budgets and want category-level audience data and omnichannel broadcasting. Without the data infrastructure and audience segmentation to prove reach and relevance, those vendor deals stay generic, underpriced, or go to your competitor.

No ROI

You can't prove CRM ROI — so the pharmacy marketing budget stays flat.

Without attribution connecting email campaigns to in-branch dispensing, prescription uplift, or OTC basket growth, marketing decisions default to intuition. If the CFO can't see the link between a loyalty campaign and last quarter's revenue, the CRM budget will always lose to a physical store refit.
